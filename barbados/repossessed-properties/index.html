Registro Housepro

What is a Secondary Mortgage Sale?

The transference of the enforceability rights under a Mortgage and Further Charges (if any) of a loan to a third party is what we call a Secondary Mortgage Sale. Generally speaking the loans are in arrears due to the lack of payments of the borrowers and depending on the judicial status of the loan, the purchase and sale of the rights enforced through the court system is allowed.

 These type of transactions are not that common in the Caribbean, but Secondary Mortgage Sales have proven to be a good investment because it has attractive prices, offers significant discounts, and the acquisition of these rights is well below the value of the property that represents the final guarantee of the mortgage loan.

What is a repossessed asset?

Houses, land, both vacant and commercial or industrial lots that clearing banks and portfolio recovery companies, such as ourselves, possess resulting from the enforcing of the mortgage as an outcome of the default from the customer. Presented as a property for sale instead of the collateral that it used to be.

The repossessed assets that are promoted and marketed by Ascendancy are in the process of being regularized for sale; which means that in some cases the properties are still in the process of being vacated.

It must be noted that on average the difference in prices between a new home and a repossessed one is between 20% and 30%, considering a well-maintained property.
