FAQ`S ASCENDANCY ST LUCIA

Why is Scotiabank selling my mortgage to Ascendancy?

Through the sale to Ascendancy, who specialize in distressed asset management, alternate financing solutions can be made  be available for customers.

Will the terms of my mortgage change?

This transaction does not affect the terms set out in the Mortgage Deed.

Who is Ascendancy?

Ascendancy St. Lucia is a private asset management firm that manages distressed properties through strategic alliances created to boost the real estate sector. Ascendancy will offer more flexible financing solutions to customers outside of limited solutions which may be offered through a bank. Solutions may include refinancing, rental arrangements, and liquidating with a discount.

Will Ascendancy take over my property?

Upon Ascendancy assuming management and servicing all correspondence and inquiries concerning your mortgage loan must be made directly to Ascendancy St. Lucia or will be directed to Ascendancy St. Lucia.

What will happen to other products I have with Scotiabank?

This transaction will not impact any other accounts maintained with Scotiabank.

Is Scotiabank no longer interested in my business?

Scotiabank will continue to work to make you financially better off and where possible, create solutions that will work for you.

Is Scotiabank leaving St.Lucia?

Scotiabank is not leaving St. Lucia. It has a long history in St. Lucia, and will continue to focus on providing better service to our customers; ensuring that they receive the right advice and solutions to meet their needs.

Why is Scotiabank selling its assets to Ascendancy?

Through the sale to Ascendancy, who specializes in distressed asset management, alternate financing solutions will be available for customers. Through this transaction, the Bank reduces its delinquency levels, improves it risk profile and expense levels which should increase access to credit in a challenged economic environment.

What will happen to these properties and property owners?

The ownership of the affected mortgage loans has been sold and assigned to Ascendancy St. Lucia. Ascendancy St. Lucia will commence management and servicing of the loans and will offer more flexible alternatives to satisfying the debt owed.

What is a Secondary Mortgage Sale?

The transference of the enforceability rights under a Mortgage and Further Charges (if any) of a loan to a third party is what we call a Secondary Mortgage Sale. Generally speaking the loans are in arrears due to the lack of payments of the borrowers and depending on the judicial status of the loan, the purchase and sale of the rights enforced through the court system is allowed.

 These type of transactions are not that common in the Caribbean, but Secondary Mortgage Sales have proven to be a good investment because it has attractive prices, offers significant discounts, and the acquisition of these rights is well below the value of the property that represents the final guarantee of the mortgage loan.

What is a repossessed asset?

Houses, land, both vacant and commercial or industrial lots that clearing banks and portfolio recovery companies, such as ourselves, possess resulting from the enforcing of the mortgage as an outcome of the default from the customer. Presented as a property for sale instead of the collateral that it used to be.

The repossessed assets that are promoted and marketed by Ascendancy are in the process of being regularized for sale; which means that in some cases the properties are still in the process of being vacated.

It must be noted that on average the difference in prices between a new home and a repossessed one is between 20% and 30%, considering a well-maintained property.
